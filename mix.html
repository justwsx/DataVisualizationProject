pie_chart 2. Why Can't We Just Quit Fossil Fuels?

It seems obvious: if fossil fuels are bad, we should stop using them. But the data shows we are still addictedâ€”over 80% of our energy comes from Coal, Oil, and Gas. The reason is physics and convenience. Fossil fuels are incredibly energy-dense and easy to transport. You can store a pile of coal anywhere for months. You can fill a car with gas in 3 minutes and drive for hours.

Renewables are different. The sun doesn't always shine, and the wind doesn't always blow. To replace the "Old Giants" shown in the first chart, we don't just need solar panels; we need massive batteries and new power grids, which takes time and money to build. However, the Market Share chart reveals a slow victory: renewables are capturing almost all *new* growth, gradually pushing the fossils into a corner.
