history 1. Why Do We Need So Much Power?

Look at the consumption line below: it never stops climbing. Why? The answer is human progress. Since 1990, billions of people in China, India, and Southeast Asia have escaped extreme poverty. When people get richer, the first thing they do is buy appliances: refrigerators to keep food fresh, washing machines to save time, and air conditioning to survive the heat.

This creates a massive dilemma shown in the Inequality Map. The rich countries (in the North) have stopped growing their energy use because they are already developed. But the developing world (in the South) is just getting started. They have a right to the same quality of life we enjoy, but if they fuel their growth with coal and oil like we did, the climate will collapse. This is the central problem of our time.
