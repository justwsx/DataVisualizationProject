How the World Uses Energy

Energy is what makes our modern life possible. From charging your phone to running big factories, everything we do requires power. This dashboard helps you see where that power comes from and how our habits are changing over time.

Right now, we are in the middle of a big shift. People still need a lot of energy to live and grow, but we are slowly moving away from old fuels like coal and oil. Below, you can explore the data to see how different countries are starting to grow their economies while trying to use cleaner energy.

history 1. Is Energy Demand Growing?

The short answer is yes. Since 1990, the world has started using much more energy. This happens because the global population is growing and more people are moving out of poverty. When people have more money, they use more electricity for their homes, cars, and jobs.

If you look at the Global Map, you will see that energy isn't used the same way everywhere. Richer countries in the North use a lot of energy per person, while many developing countries are just now starting to catch up. The big question for the future is: how can everyone have enough power without hurting the environment?

pie_chart 2. What Types of Fuel Do We Use?

Even though we talk a lot about solar panels and wind turbines, the world still runs mostly on "fire." This means we still burn a lot of coal, oil, and gas. These are called fossil fuels.

The charts below show the "Energy Mix." You can see that fossil fuels are still the biggest part of our energy. However, the green section for Renewables is getting bigger every single year. It is a slow change, but it is definitely happening.

analytics 4. Economy, Growth, and the Planet

In the past, people thought that to make a country richer, you had to burn more fuel and create more pollution. But today, we are learning to do things differently. We are becoming more efficient, which means we can create more wealth using less energy.

The Carbon Intensity chart below shows something very important: we are producing less CO2 for every unit of energy we use. This is great news! It means that we can grow our economies and improve our lives while doing less damage to the Earth.

Summary of What We Learned

trending_up

Green Energy is Growing Fast:

Renewables like solar and wind are not just "backup" options anymore. They are growing so fast because they are now some of the cheapest ways to make electricity. In many places, they are already outperforming coal and gas.

public

Demand is Moving to Asia:

While countries like the US and many in Europe are using about the same amount of energy (or even less), countries like China and India are growing very quickly. The world's energy future will mostly depend on how these regions choose to develop.

eco

We Still Use Fossil Fuels:

Even though green energy is growing, we still use a lot of coal and oil. The data shows that we are *adding* green energy to the world, but we haven't fully *replaced* the old fuels yet. We still have work to do.

bolt

We are Getting Better and Smarter:

We are learning how to create more wealth with less energy. This "Efficiency" is very important. It means we can have a better quality of life without necessarily polluting more. The goal is to keep this trend going.
