The Pulse of Our Planet

Energy is the lifeblood of our modern world. Think about your day: from the coffee machine in the morning to the streetlights at night, energy makes it all possible. For over a hundred years, we have built our world by burning coal, oil, and gas. This helped us grow, but it also changed our climate.

Now, we are in the middle of a massive shift. We are learning how to keep the lights on while moving toward cleaner sources like the sun and the wind. This dashboard is your guide to that journey. Explore the data below to see how our habits are changing and which countries are leading the way to a better future.

history 1. The World’s Growing Hunger for Power

Are we using more energy? The simple answer is yes. Since 1990, global energy use has nearly doubled. This isn't just because there are more people, but because more people are moving into the middle class. When people have better lives, they use more electricity for their homes, schools, and hospitals.

However, as you can see in the Global Map below, energy isn't used equally. Wealthier countries use much more energy per person than developing nations. The big question for the next generation is: how can we give everyone enough power without following the "dirty" path of the past?

pie_chart 2. What Exactly Are We Burning?

Despite all the talk about solar panels and wind farms, the world still runs mostly on "fire." This means we still burn a lot of coal, oil, and gas—often called fossil fuels. These fuels are reliable, but they are also the main cause of pollution.

The charts below show the "Energy Mix." You can see that fossil fuels are still the biggest part of our energy "pie." However, the green slice for Renewables is getting bigger every single year. It is a slow change, but it is definitely moving in the right direction.

autorenew 3. The Renewable Revolution

This is the most hopeful part of the story. Clean energy, like wind and solar, is growing faster than any other source in history. Because technology is getting better, it has become the cheapest way to make electricity in most countries.

In the charts below, you can see how fast we are adopting these new technologies. When we combine Renewables with Nuclear power, we see a path toward a world that produces much less CO2. Many countries are now proving that you can have a strong economy without needing a lot of coal.

analytics 4. Economy: Doing More with Less

In the past, people thought that to make a country richer, you had to pollute more. But today, we are proving that isn't true. We are becoming more **efficient**, which means we can create more wealth using less energy.

The Carbon Intensity chart below shows something very important: we are producing less CO2 for every unit of energy we use. This is great news! It means we are learning to live better lives while doing less damage to the Earth.

public 5. The World is Changing Directions

Energy use tells us a lot about global power. In the past, the US and Europe were the biggest consumers. But today, the focus has shifted to Asia. China and India are growing fast and need massive amounts of energy to build their futures.

This means the future of our planet depends on the choices made in these regions. If they choose green energy instead of coal, the whole world wins. The charts below show this historic shift between continents and the countries that use the most power today.

Summary: What Have We Learned?

trending_up

Green energy is cheaper now:

We are not just using solar and wind because it's good for the Earth, but because it's now the cheapest way to make power. This economic shift is driving change faster than any law could.

public

Asia is the new engine of demand:

Most new energy demand is coming from China and India. The future of our climate depends on the choices made in these regions more than anywhere else.

eco

We need to replace, not just add:

Currently, we are adding green energy to meet new needs, but we haven't stopped using coal yet. To save the climate, we need to start shutting down old plants permanently.

bolt

We are getting smarter:

We produce more wealth with less energy than we did 30 years ago. Getting smarter about how we use power is our best weapon against climate change.
