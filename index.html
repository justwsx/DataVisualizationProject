The Energy that moves the World

Everything we do requires energy. From charging your phone to heating our homes, energy is the engine of our daily lives. In recent years, however, we have realized that the way we produce this energy must change to protect our planet.

This page will help you understand how over 8 billion people are trying to transition from "old" polluting sources (like coal and oil) to "new" clean sources (like sun and wind). Explore the charts to see who is improving and how far we still have to go.

history 1. Are we using more and more energy?

Yes, over the last 30 years world energy consumption has almost doubled. This happens because the population increases and more and more people can finally afford appliances, cars, and modern tools they didn't have before.

However, as you can see from the map below, energy is not well distributed: the richest countries use a huge amount of energy, while many others still have too little. The challenge of the future is to give energy to everyone, but without polluting as we have done in the past.

pie_chart 2. What are we "burning"?

Despite all the talk about solar panels, today the world still runs on fire. Oil, coal, and gas are still the "giants" that move almost everything. They are easy to use sources, but they are also the most polluting.

Look at the charts below: the green "slice" of Renewable Energies is growing, but it is still small compared to fossil fuels. We are trying to change habits, but it is a job that takes time.

autorenew 3. The Clean Energy revolution

Here comes the good news. Clean technologies (like sun and wind) are growing faster than any other source in history. Today they cost much less than ten years ago and have become the cheapest choice in many countries.

In the charts below you see this "green explosion." If we combine renewables with nuclear energy, we get a mix that does not produce smoke and does not heat the planet. It is the fastest path to a cleaner future.

analytics 4. Becoming rich without polluting

For a long time we thought that to grow the economy we necessarily had to pollute more. But today we are showing that this is not true. We can produce more wealth by using energy more intelligently.

Look at the "Carbon Intensity" chart: it shows how much smoke we produce for every drop of energy we use. The line is going down! It means we are learning to do the same things in a cleaner way. It is a challenge between our intelligence and our consumption.

public 5. The center of gravity is shifting East

In the past, Europe and the United States were the masters of energy. Today, however, everything is changing. China and India have become the new protagonists: they consume a lot because they are growing fast.

This means that the future of our planet depends on the choices that will be made in Asia. If these countries choose the sun and wind instead of coal, we will all win. In the charts below you can clearly see this historic overtake between the various continents.

Green costs less:

public

Asia decides our future:

China and India are the new engines of the world. The decisions made there will have an impact on the entire planet, much more than those made in Europe.

eco

We add, do not replace:

Careful: we are adding green energy to cover new consumption, but we have not yet stopped using coal. To save the climate, we must start "turning off" the old, not just "turning on" the new.

bolt

We are smarter:

We know how to produce more wealth using less energy compared to 30 years ago. This efficiency is our secret weapon to live well without destroying the Earth.
