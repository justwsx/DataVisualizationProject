Global Energy Consumption & GDP Analytics

Interactive Dashboard with 10 Visualizations

1. Global Energy Intensity (3D Globe)

Explore the geospatial distribution of energy intensity across the planet. Brighter colors highlight regions with higher energy consumption relative to their GDP, visually emphasizing the efficiency gap between developing and developed economies.

2. GDP vs. Energy Consumption (Bubble Chart)

Analyze the correlation between economic output (GDP) and energy consumption. The bubble size represents population, allowing you to identify outliers—such as highly efficient economies or energy-intensive developing nations—at a glance.

3. Energy Mix Evolution (Stacked Area)

Track the historical transition of a specific country's energy portfolio. Observe how the share of renewables, nuclear energy, and fossil fuels has shifted over decades in response to policy changes, technological advancements, and economic needs.

4. Energy Sources Share (Donut)

Get a snapshot of the current energy matrix breakdown. This visualization separates total consumption into specific sources (Solar, Wind, Oil, Coal), revealing the immediate dependency on carbon-intensive fuels versus clean energy alternatives.

5. Pareto Analysis (Top Consumers)

Applying the 80/20 rule to global energy data. This chart highlights the few key countries responsible for the vast majority of global consumption or CO2 emissions, helping to focus decarbonization efforts where they will have the highest impact.

6. Energy Intensity Heatmap (Top 20 Countries)

Visualize the temporal evolution of energy intensity among the world's top consumers. Darker shades represent higher intensity, making it easy to spot trends of improvement (lightening colors) or stagnation across different time periods.

7. Energy Profile Comparison (Radar)

Directly compare the multidimensional energy profiles of two nations. This view contrasts metrics like renewable share, fossil fuel dependency, and per capita consumption, benchmarking performance against international peers.

8. Global Energy Distribution (Treemap)

Understand the hierarchical structure of global energy use. The area size represents total consumption, grouped by continent or region, offering an immediate view of regional dominance and geopolitical energy disparities.

9. Top 10 Energy Consumers Over Time

Watch the rise and fall of global energy giants. This animated race chart visualizes the rapid industrialization of emerging economies and the steady demand of established powers throughout the 20th and 21st centuries.

10. Energy Flow: Sources to Regions (Sankey)

Trace the complex flow of energy from primary sources (left) to end-use regions (right). This flow diagram illustrates the magnitude of energy transfer and helps visualize global supply chain dynamics and regional dependencies.
