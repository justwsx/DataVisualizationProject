autorenew 3. The Tipping Point: Why Green is Winning

For decades, green energy was a nice idea, but it was too expensive. That has completely changed. Look at the "hockey stick" curve in the Adoption chart. Why is it shooting up now? Because of economies of scale. China poured money into manufacturing solar panels, causing the price to crash by 90% in ten years.

Now, in most places, building a new solar farm is cheaper than burning coal. That is the engine of the transition: it's no longer just about saving the planet; it's about saving money. When we combine this cheap renewable power with Nuclear (which provides stable, always-on power without CO2), we finally have a realistic blueprint to replace the old fossil fuel machine.
